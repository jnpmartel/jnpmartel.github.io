If you have ever been curious to know who your doctoral \[grand-\]*fathers are, and you are lucky enough to be in STEM,
then you probably consulted the [Mathematics Genealogy Project](https://genealogy.math.ndsu.nodak.edu/) (if not, you 
should and you might realize you are in the same line as Euler, Lagrange, Fourier, Dirichlet, Lipschitz or Hilbert!)
Interestingly, I am not one of those 118 877 grand children Euler had. Instead I am part of what seems to be a relatively 
small (american) connected component of the graph! Also my tree is not very wide (most students in it, had a single 
advisor apart from 1. Joe Goodman, and 2. Vannevar Bush).
